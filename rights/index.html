Lead asset · Trilogy · Complete

Piratesse del Mediterraneo

A completed adult historical-fantasy trilogy set in 17th-century Spanish-occupied Sardinia. Three books, voice-forward, dual-POV with register shifts. Published in Italian by Acheron Books; in French by Hikaya. Italian audio with Audible. English-language rights worldwide: available.

She is a Sardinian fisherwoman who dreams of stealing a galleon, becoming a pirate, and escaping a violent husband. He is the Grand Inquisitor of the Crown of Spain. And women like her, he burns alive.

The Adventures of Amina al-Sirafi meets The Bone Ships, with the Inquisition-era darkness of Bardugo's The Familiar, and for readers who loved the heist-and-found-family of The Lies of Locke Lamora.

In 1630s Sardinia, being a witch is a death sentence. So is not being one, and acting like one. Fiammetta should have just kept quiet and obeyed, like all the other women on her dismal island. Instead, for years, she has been preparing her escape: a galleon bought in secret from a Genoese merchant, a crew of battered wives, and the wild dream of becoming the most feared pirate captain in the Mediterranean.

When Stellina, a little girl possessed by a Zipa spirit, promises to lead her to the buried treasure of a legendary Sardinian pirate, Fiammetta thinks she has found her chance. But Ambrosio, the brooding witch-hunter of the Holy Inquisition, is on the girl's trail too. Now Fiammetta and her ragtag crew have one choice: reach the treasure before the Spaniards do, or pay with their freedom and their blood.

Across three books the trilogy widens from comic-bawdy first-person picaresque into a dual-POV historical fantasy in the register-shifting tradition of Joe Abercrombie's First Law. Fiammetta's irreverent voice sits alongside darker chapters as the Inquisition closes in, the magical economy of Spanish gold reveals its cost, and the trilogy's feminist undertones and depictions of gender violence sharpen into focus.

About the author

Titania Blesh

Titania Blesh is an Italian science-fiction and fantasy author, traditionally published in Italy and France with audio editions on Audible Italia. She writes for adults and young adults across fantasy, historical fantasy, and science fiction. Her work has been recognized with the Trofeo Cassiopea 2022 (Italy's adult-SF award) and as a finalist for the Trofeo Cittadella 2021 (Italy's adult-fantasy award).

Her completed adult historical-fantasy trilogy, Piratesse del Mediterraneo, was published in Italian by Acheron Books and in French by Hikaya. Her short fiction has appeared in English in Mirror World Publishing's anthology Against All Odds (2025). She has nine completed novels – four published, five awaiting first publication – and is actively seeking representation and acquisitions for English-language editions.

Upcoming appearances: Les Imaginales (Épinal, 2026); Les Intergalactiques (Lyon, 2026); Étrange Grande (2026).

Past appearances: Lucca Comics & Games (2022, panel on writing historical fantasy in Italy); Salone Internazionale del Libro (Turin, 2021); Stranimondi (Milan, 2021).
